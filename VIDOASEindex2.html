FILM NUMBER 2
A study of a man called Maarten Arntzenius. Please sit back and relax to let this man sink in. In first instance, he might look a bit normal in a weird sort of uninteresting banal sort of way. But his syntax and psychological state really got me fascinated. And I also really appreciate his willingness to discuss and share without  judging me for being curious and wanting to know all these things.

I met him in the mediamarkt when I was thinking about buying a device. He was working there and I asked him for advice. He started asking me philosophical questions. I wanted to film him. In the mediamarkt it's not allowed to film. (I had a problem once with a security guard inside Mediamarkt for filming the staircase, he was rather aggressive,). So I proposed to meet at his house. This film is the result of just 3 meetings over a 2 month time span.

His main topic is women and what they are doing to men. That's what he talks about mostly. He is writing a book titled: 'The Amazon Woman 2.0'. During the filming session his theories and thoughts about woman are sort of supplemented (or countered, tested) by 3 elements and happenings that happen to take place during the period of me filming him.

Element 1: HIS MOTHER
I get the impression that his relationship with his mother is very telling about the way he relates to women so I start filming her as well. Then I discover that she is about to end her life. I'm interested in the way Maarten deals with this.

Element 2: A NEW DAUGHTER
He also discovers that he has a daughter of 21, and he feels semi-betrayed by the mother who never told him about this.

Element 3:
Finding  new love. Falling in and out of love with various women.

FILM NUMBER 1
ANCIENT AMATEURS. This film was tailor made to be shown as an art install in the Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam (the Bureau project space they have). It follows a small group of porn actors in their attempt to make an art film.

And in their attempt to make something artistic, they start using strategies that are very similar to what most professional artists do: trying to discover what is 'real' or 'essential' about something and conveying that within the artpiece.

In this case, the porn-actors conclude that porn is sort of fake because it isn't about reproduction and creating life and making babies. In order to reconnect to some sort of essence, they make a film in which their pornography does have consequences: Babies are born. The dramatic aspect is that every baby seems to be immediately trapped into a rigourous life circle. Once a baby is born, it is immediately impregnated in order to reproduce and die.

The film shows 2 life cycles and one dying event. But it is also at the same time a making-of documentary.
