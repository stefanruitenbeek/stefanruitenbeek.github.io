THE ARTIST AS ARCHAEOLOGIST AND WHY THATS A BAD IDEA

Looking at work by Saskia Van Imhoff and Alicja Kwade at De Appel arts centre, Amsterdam, March 2016

A lot of artist these days present themselves as a sort of 'discoverers' of contemporary culture. They present there findings as precious artifacts, powerful and contemplative in their meaningless ambiguity. A pseudo scientific objectivity gives these wornout acts of appropriation a sort of genteel legitimate aura. In this video we look at the work of 2 of these  archeo-artists: we strongly disaprove of the work -even within the genre of archaeo-art it seems very weak- and we also explain why a government funded institute with its army of curators pays attention to this genre. Archaeo art is silly, but it is part of our world and we keep running in to archaeo-art when we walk into museums and project spaces. This videoclip is the result of taking a videocamera during one of these visits.

ANCIENT AMATEURS
Language: Dutch, English Subs
2011-2012

CONTAINS HARDCORE SEX

This film was made to be shown as an art install in the Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam (the Bureau project space they have). It follows a small group of porn actors in their attempt to make an art film.

And in their attempt to make something artistic, they start using strategies that are very similar to what most professional artists do: trying to discover what is 'real' or 'essential' about something and conveying that within the artpiece.

In this case, the porn-actors conclude that porn is sort of fake because it isn't about reproduction and creating life and making babies. In order to reconnect to some sort of essence, they make a film in which their pornography does have consequences: Babies are born. The dramatic aspect is that every baby seems to be immediately trapped into a rigourous life circle. Once a baby is born, it is immediately impregnated in order to reproduce and die.

The film shows 2 life cycles and one dying event. But it is also at the same time a making-of documentary
